The Dearborn River

Home Streamflow Fishing

The Dearborn river is a smaller mountain stream in central Montana. It begins in the Scapegoat Wilderness, and becomes the full river at the confluence with the North and South forks.
Along the headwaters, it is known for extremely cold water, deep pools, and beautiful secluded canyons.
Locals enjoy hiking Devils Glen, which follows the Dearborn through these canyons and provides breathtaking scenery.
Further downstream, the river becomes floatable in rafts, canoes, and kayaks during high water.
