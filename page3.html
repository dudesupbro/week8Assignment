Fishing the Dearborn is a fickle bitch.
As stated on Streamflow, the river is only floatable for a very small part of the summer.
During these times, water may be too high for good fishing.
However, during lower flowes, one may fish any of the deep pools, riffle patterns, side foam, or deep channels abutting rocks.
Spinning rods work great with just about any lure: panther martin spinners, rapallas, or normal red and white patterns.
Flies include streamers, Bitch Creek special, Mayflies, and most importantly, whatever is hatching that day!
Talk to some locals in Cascade or Craig and they will let you know which hatch to mimic.


A beautiful brown trout.
Brown, Rainbow, Cutthroat, Mountain Whitefish, and Mountain Suckers abound.
